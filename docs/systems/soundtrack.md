# Music & Soundtrack

*Status: direction only, nothing built. This is the **music** page - separate from the technical [engine audio](audio.md) system, which is about how the car *sounds*, not what plays over (or in) the game.*

## The vibe

Old-school **synthwave rock** - synth-driven but with real guitar bite and drive. Tentative, but the touchstone is **Dance with the Dead** (darksynth + heavy guitar): propulsive, retro, a little menacing. It fits the premise - an anachronistic muscle car tearing through a dead megastructure wants exactly that energy.

## The car radio (music is diegetic)

The core decision: **music plays from an in-car radio the crew switches on** - it lives *in the world*, not laid over the top ([decision](../project/decisions.md)). It's the same logic as the [engine being the co-op living room's soundtrack](audio.md) and the [colour being diegetic](../level-design/world-structure.md): plugged in = alive = the radio comes on.

Why it earns its place:

- Reinforces the premise - the car is power, life, colour, **and** the music. It's all one thing switching on.
- It's controllable in-fiction: the crew turns it up, changes the station, kills it. Music becomes something players *do*, not just hear.
- Room to play: does the radio duck or cut in tense moments? Does deep-zone interference chew at the signal like the [drone tether](../level-design/world-structure.md)? All on the table.

## Open questions

- **Where and when does music play?** Hub only, whole runs, combat stingers, or sparse ambient in the tunnels? Undecided - and it collides with pacing: the tunnels' target feel is *headlights through fog*, which may want tense ambient, not a banging track. Music placement and the [scale-dread pacing](../level-design/setting-and-scale.md) have to be designed together ([The Tunnels](../level-design/tunnels.md)).
- **All radio, or radio + score?** Pure diegetic radio, or a non-diegetic score too for set-pieces like a landmark fight?
- **Original vs licensed.** Synthwave-rock references imply real artists; originals in that style keep licensing clean.
