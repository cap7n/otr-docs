# Landmark Enemies & Bosses

The direction for OTR's *big* threats — the opposite of the interchangeable [roster](enemies.md). Fewer mobs, more one-of-a-kind encounters that need a **method** to kill, not just a health bar emptied ([enemy direction decision](../project/decisions.md)). The locked-gate weak-point is the seed this grows from.

## What a landmark is

- **Zone-specific big bosses.** Each zone can carry its own landmark threat, matched to its era and theme.
- **No type is off-limits.** Organic creatures, robots and machines, and **the structure itself** (a hostile automated system, a facility that fights back) are all fair game — the anachronistic zones mean any style has a home ([World & Structure](../level-design/world-structure.md)). Tied to the fiction, but broad enough to experiment freely.
- **Usually the climax.** A landmark is the set-piece a [route/zone is built toward](../level-design/tunnels.md) — though not every zone needs one.

## The rule: a method, not a health bar

A landmark isn't "shoot until it dies." It has a *way* to be beaten — a weakness, a setup, a sequence — and finding and executing that way **is** the fight. The flavors we'll use:

- **Co-op-gated takedowns** — the kill only exists if both seats act. *Archetype: a tall four-legged creature — the co-pilot wraps the [harpoon](weapons-combat.md) around its legs to trip it, and while it's down someone shoots the exposed head.* One kill made of two actions, straight out of the [co-op pillar](../co-op-design.md).
- **Lure into hazards** — the arena fights alongside you: bait the thing onto a collapsing floor, into a steam blast, off a ledge, or into a wall-drive-only path it can't follow ([hazards](../systems/hazards.md)).
- **Weak-points** — armored until a structural or glowing point is exposed and hit (the locked-gate machinery already does this).
- **Phases / sequence** — behavior shifts that demand a new answer at each stage.

## Keeping them landmarks

- **Authored, not spawned in packs.** The moment there are two of them, it's a big mob, not a landmark. The "you'll remember this one" feeling is the whole point.
- **The [two-seat test](../co-op-design.md) applies, harder.** If one player can solo it, it isn't a landmark yet.
- **The method must be readable in-fight** — telegraphs, exposed weak-points, obvious hazard bait — so a silent duo can figure it out without a wiki (coordination without a mic).

## Status

- The current boss (500 HP, phase-based tracking laser) is **benched — it needs a full remake**; don't build a route climax on it. Meanwhile, climaxes use hordes-in-caverns and set-pieces ([roster](enemies.md)).
- Landmark encounters are a **direction, not built.** The first one rides along with the [first authored zone](../project/roadmap.md).
