    Landmark Enemies & Bosses¶

    The direction for OTR's big threats - the opposite of the interchangeable roster. Fewer mobs, more one-of-a-kind encounters that need a method to kill, not just a health bar emptied (enemy direction decision). The locked-gate weak-point is the seed this grows from.

    What a landmark is¶

    • Zone-specific big bosses. Each zone can carry its own landmark threat, matched to its era and theme.
    • No type is off-limits. Organic creatures, robots and machines, and the structure itself (a hostile automated system, a facility that fights back) are all fair game - the anachronistic zones mean any style has a home (World & Structure). Tied to the fiction, but broad enough to experiment freely.
    • Usually the climax. A landmark is the set-piece a route/zone is built toward - though not every zone needs one.

    The rule: a method, not a health bar¶

    A landmark isn't "shoot until it dies." It has a way to be beaten - a weakness, a setup, a sequence - and finding and executing that way is the fight. The flavors we'll use:

    • Co-op-gated takedowns - the kill only exists if both seats act. Archetype: a tall four-legged creature - the co-pilot wraps the harpoon around its legs to trip it, and while it's down someone shoots the exposed head. One kill made of two actions, straight out of the co-op pillar.
    • Lure into hazards - the arena fights alongside you: bait the thing onto a collapsing floor, into a steam blast, off a ledge, or into a wall-drive-only path it can't follow (hazards).
    • Weak-points - armored until a structural or glowing point is exposed and hit (the locked-gate machinery already does this).
    • Phases / sequence - behavior shifts that demand a new answer at each stage.

    Keeping them landmarks¶

    • Authored, not spawned in packs. The moment there are two of them, it's a big mob, not a landmark. The "you'll remember this one" feeling is the whole point.
    • The two-seat test applies, harder. If one player can solo it, it isn't a landmark yet.
    • The method must be readable in-fight - telegraphs, exposed weak-points, obvious hazard bait - so a silent duo can figure it out without a wiki (coordination without a mic).

    Status¶

    • The current boss (500 HP, phase-based tracking laser) is benched - it needs a full remake; don't build a route climax on it. Meanwhile, climaxes use hordes-in-caverns and set-pieces (roster).
    • Landmark encounters are a direction, not built. The first one rides along with the first authored zone.
