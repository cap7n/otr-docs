    Music & Soundtrack¶

    Status: direction only, nothing built. This is the music page - separate from the technical engine audio system, which is about how the car sounds, not what plays over (or in) the game.

    The vibe¶

    Old-school synthwave rock - synth-driven but with real guitar bite and drive. Tentative, but the touchstone is Dance with the Dead (darksynth + heavy guitar): propulsive, retro, a little menacing. It fits the premise - an anachronistic muscle car tearing through a dead megastructure wants exactly that energy.

    The car radio (music is diegetic)¶

    The core decision: music plays from an in-car radio the crew switches on - it lives in the world, not laid over the top (decision). It's the same logic as the engine being the co-op living room's soundtrack and the colour being diegetic: plugged in = alive = the radio comes on.

    Why it earns its place:

    • Reinforces the premise - the car is power, life, colour, and the music. It's all one thing switching on.
    • It's controllable in-fiction: the crew turns it up, changes the station, kills it. Music becomes something players do, not just hear.
    • Room to play: does the radio duck or cut in tense moments? Does deep-zone interference chew at the signal like the drone tether? All on the table.

    Open questions¶

    • Where and when does music play? Hub only, whole runs, combat stingers, or sparse ambient in the tunnels? Undecided - and it collides with pacing: the tunnels' target feel is headlights through fog, which may want tense ambient, not a banging track. Music placement and the scale-dread pacing have to be designed together (The Tunnels).
    • All radio, or radio + score? Pure diegetic radio, or a non-diegetic score too for set-pieces like a landmark fight?
    • Original vs licensed. Synthwave-rock references imply real artists; originals in that style keep licensing clean.

    Other (non-engine) sound¶

    The turret, screech, explosion, and UI SFX still predate the engine-audio system and use one-shot players - a consistency and bus-routing pass is a someday (engine audio open items, backlog). This page will grow to hold the whole sound direction as it firms up.
