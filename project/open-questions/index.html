    Open Questions¶

    Things not yet decided. When one gets answered, move it to the Decision Log with its why.

    Blocking level design¶

    1. Saving & stakes model WIP the big one¶

    The world structure answered the run's shape (expeditions through shifting zones, exits as finish lines, battery as pressure). What's left is what death and saving mean, and that defines every stake in the game:

    • Save/load: dying reloads the last save; everything since is gone. Simple, but "going back in time" sits oddly with a world that shifts forward.
    • Roguelite-ish: no rewinding, death has forward-moving consequences (lose the cargo, lose the zone, wake somewhere else, something is different). Fits the fiction better; needs more design.

    Sub-questions that hang on this: when does saving happen (garage-only? rare in-world docks?), what exactly is lost on death, and when loot becomes "banked". The junker proposal below survives either answer.

    2. The junker start Idea (proposal, survives any saving model)¶

    Start with a weak car; abilities and power are bought as CAR_MOD items. Rule: underpowered must never mean unresponsive, weak stats with good feel.

    Implementation readiness (checked 2026-07): the ability architecture is already modular; ~a day of work: (1) unlocked flag + early-return per controller, peer-synced; (2) actual CAR_MOD items (category exists as enum only); (3) a save file (nothing persists to disk yet, needed regardless of the saving model chosen).

    Onboarding refinement (2026-07-07, on the table, not decided): instead of opening on a weak car, open on a good one and then break it - the intro car's engine fails and the crew installs a junker engine - so the player's first taste is good driving, not a bad one. Car-tuning presets may be saved per engine so a swap isn't a full re-tune. Direction only.

    Design¶

    • Idea Long-term direction: expeditions go out and come home, but where does it all go? Escape the structure? Reach an edge? Ever-deeper mastery with no promised outside? Undefined for now, and fine to leave until the core loop grips.
    • Idea Is the vastness beautiful or hostile? The gray-to-color intro suggests the answer trends vivid-when-powered; the palette question is now really "how vivid, and does it vary per zone theme?" - and Pratina hands it a lever: a zone's vividness could read its ripeness state (fresh/vivid, peak/harvested, spoiled/dangerous). Still open until that aesthetic mapping is chosen.
    • Idea Is cargo space limited? If yes, "what do we keep" becomes a co-op negotiation.
    • Idea What does running dry do? Fuel's source is now decided - fuel = refined Pratina, bought via the salvager resupply or scavenged mid-run (Pratina, moved to the Decision Log). What's left is the mechanic: does running dry strand you mid-zone, or just end the run? A tuning detail, still open.
    • Blocked Car damage & repair model: the car has HP, takes damage, and is repaired in the hub, and the wear shader is built to show it - but the actual numbers (how much HP, how repair is priced, what a beaten car costs) aren't specified anywhere, and they're tangled with what death costs (question 1).
    • Idea In-game comms vs. coordination-by-action: we don't assume voice chat, so co-op is meant to read through coupled actions and diegetic tells (Co-op Design Principles). Open: does the game still ship explicit tools (the callout/ping system, a shared marker) as a safety net, or lean fully on action + world-reading?
    • Idea Driver's built damage tools vs. the no-damage rule: the flamethrower (50 DPS) and 4-tube rocket pot are built on the driver, but the combat split puts all damage on the co-pilot. Reconcile: move them to the co-pilot, reframe the flamethrower as a non-damage push / area-denial tool, or cut them (Weapons & Combat). (Direction decided 2026-07-14, see the Backlog "Now" list; retire this once the move ships.)
    • Idea Rebar/harpoon launcher - firing mechanism: crossbow, gunpowder, air-pressure off the car, or railgun. Undecided; it drives the sound, the reload feel, and the fiction (Weapons & Combat).
    • Idea R key conflict: wall-drive toggle vs. rocket pot fire, both on R, one has to move before wall-drive is re-enabled. (Jump input resolved: it shipped on Q.)
    • Idea Robotic insectoid "enemy" drones: whether to implement them as an enemy type at all - undecided. If yes, still open what they'd do (scout, swarm, harasser) and how they fit the combat split and no-damage rule.
